In my free time, I enjoy going to the gym, trying new resturants and cafes, cooking, and mixed martial arts for fun. I've made it my mission to find the best cafes in NYC so here are a few of my favorites.

SEY Coffee

SEY Coffee is a small cafe with only one location in Brooklyn, NY. The space is alive with people and plants with plenty of natural light for a more natural environment. The inside is constructed with wood and concrete to give the cafe more of a natural feel.
A con for though is that the cafe does not allow laptops on weekends and is limited on weekdays; however, I do see the benefit in disconnecting from technology for a second and just living in the moment. Seats are tough to find though since the cafe is usually busy on weekends, so it may be hard to sit down and enjoy your drink.

Blue Bottle Coffee

Blue Bottle is one of my favoriate coffee shops and is probably my go to pick when it comes to cafes. Their store locations are consitently designed well with a lot of though put into the atmosphere of the cafe. Their coffee is also top notch and ethically sourced which justifies the price.
Aside from the price of the coffee, I personally don't find many flaws with these cafes. They are usually great to work at and provide a nice ambiance to focus in. I love the location in NYC Chinatown since it is large and has many seats, but if I don't want to go that far they're still many locations around me.

Philz Coffee

What I love about Philz is their coffee and creations. If I had to choose a cafe that I consistently got the best coffee from, I think it might be Philz. When I had a hybrid internship in the bay my freshman summer, just about every remote day I went to the nearby Philz to work.
Philz started in California and is not that big of a chain so unfortunately, they only have locations in California and Chicago right now though I hope they'll open one in NYC soon.
